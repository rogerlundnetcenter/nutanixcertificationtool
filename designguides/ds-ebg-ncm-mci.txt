1.‚ÄãExam 
1.1 Purpose of Exam 
The Nutanix Certified Master - Multicloud Infrastructure (NCM-MCI) 6.10 exam will measure a candidate‚Äôs 
ability to analyze, evaluate, and optimize platform performance, configuration, and health. Successful 
candidates demonstrate mastery of these skills and abilities. 
The exam will also validate the candidate‚Äôs ability to remediate and evolve the platform to be in compliance 
with business requirements 
1.2 Number of Questions 
The NCM-MCI 6.10 exam consists of 17 scenarios that are to be performed in a live Nutanix multi-cluster 
environment. 
1.3 Pricing 
The cost for the NCM-MCI 6.10 exam is $300 USD. 
1.4 Passing Score 
The passing score for this exam is 3000, using a scaled scoring method. The scale is from 1000-6000. ‚Äã
Scaled scores are calculated using a mathematical formula that considers a variety of factors, including ‚Äã
the number and type of exam questions included in a specific version of the exam.  
Because this combination may vary in different versions of the same examination, scaled scores provide ‚Äã
a fair score for everyone based on the version of the exam taken. 
1.5 How Objectives Relate to Questions on the Exam 
Objectives summarize what the test is designed to measure. Objectives are developed by Exam Developers 
and Subject Matter Experts based on identified tasks that relate to the job of deploying and administering 
an AOS and Prism Central environment.  
Once the initial development process is complete, these objectives are verified using an external group of 
individuals in the actual job role. Finally, a number of questions is determined for each objective, which 
relates directly to the criticality of the task in the job role. 
1.6 Languages 
The exam is available in English. 
1.7 Time Limit 
The time limit for the exam is 180 minutes. 
1.8 Scheduling and Taking the Exam 
This exam is delivered via remote proctoring or in-person at select test centers.

receive information on how to take the exam from your location using a web browser. Because the exam ‚Äã
is remote proctored, you will be provided with a locked down, monitored, secure exam experience.  
If you select in-person testing, you will be able to select a test center near you. On the day of the exam, ‚Äã
you will need to arrive at the test center 15 minutes prior to the exam start time with a valid 
government-issued ID. 
1.9 Certification Tracks 
The NCM-MCI 6.10 exam is a core component of the Nutanix Multicloud Infrastructure track.  Passing this 
exam results in achieving the NCM-MCI 6 certification. 
The certification requires a passing score on the exam. While it is not required that you attend a course, 
Nutanix provides training that covers the objectives on the exam. Details on the recommended training 
course are provided in Section 4. 
1.10 Retake Policy 
If a candidate fails an exam on the first attempt, he or she is allowed two additional attempts. There is ‚Äã
a seven-day waiting period between attempts. Like the first attempt, these are paid for individually and 
Nutanix recommends that you allow sufficient time between attempts to be properly prepared and to 
maximize your chances for success.  
Please note: After three attempts, you will be unable to take the exam for 60 days, after which you can ‚Äã
email university.nutanix.com and request that your attempts are reset. Nutanix recommends you utilize the 
time to thoroughly review this guide and the related references and/or take the recommended training for 
this exam. 
1.11 Exam Security 
Nutanix reserves the right to refuse certifying a candidate who violates exam security policies. This includes 
copying and redistribution of exam material, using any type of study material during the exam itself, 
attempting to photograph exam items and taking an exam using a false identity. Your identity is captured 
as part of the exam registration process and must be validated before you will be allowed to take the exam.

A candidate for the Nutanix Certified Professional ‚Äì Multicloud Infrastructure 6.10 (NCM-MCI) 6.10 exam must

2. Intended Audience 
A candidate taking the NCM-MCI 6.10 exam should have approximately 3-5 years of holistic IT infrastructure 
experience and 2-3 years of Nutanix virtualization experience or applicable training. 
Successful candidates are typically senior IT staff new to Nutanix, Senior NOC/SOC escalation engineers and 
operators, and Tier 2/3 support personnel who are able to analyze business objectives and requirements, 
configure platforms to support stated business requirements, and have a high degree of familiarity with API 
calls and CLI functionalities. 
Finally, the successful candidate should be able to analyze and implement business continuity plans and 
security policies.

Storage: Take a deep dive into AOS storage services, different aspects of Acropolis Distributed

5. Live Lab Considerations 
5.1 General Lab Layout 
The NCM-MCI 6.10 exam will launch into a Windows desktop. That desktop will have Firefox, Adobe Acrobat 
Reader, Postman, and Putty installed, with the relevant links on the desktop.  The desktop will also have a 
folder with documentation and support PDFs. 
The exam scenarios will be accessed from a web browser. Each scenario will include some or all of the 
following: background, requirement(s), configuration(s), access information and credentials.  The web page 
with the questions will open automatically when the exam launches but it can also be accessed using the 
‚ÄúInstructions‚Äù link on the desktop. 
5.2 Lab Components 
The scenarios will be performed on two live AOS clusters connected to one Prism Central VM. These clusters 
are nested in the Google Cloud Platform and have a few differences compared to physical environments, so 
some suspension of disbelief is required - for instance none of the VMs will have an OS and most will not 
have hard drives or networking attached.    
The GCP deployment also prevents the 192.168.5.x internal host/CVM network from working and there will be 
an unused and powered off CVM listed on each node.  Do not make changes to any component you are not 
asked to manipulate.  Environment differences that could affect a scenario will be described in the scenario. 
The environment is located behind a NAT firewall, so the internal IPs are consistent from exam to exam, but 
from the provided workstation, they will have a different IP address.   The 172.30.x addresses will not be 
directly available from the workstation, and the environment components will only be able to use the 
172.30.0.x address range.  For example, to access the node on Cluster 1 from the Windows desktop, you will 
need to use the NATed IP to connect to the CVM and from there SSH to 172.30.0.5.

5.4 Tips and Tricks 
Here are a few suggestions to improve your exam experience: 
‚óè‚Äã
Be familiar with Prism Element pass-though from Prism Central.  This may work better in the lab 
than directly accessing PE. 
‚óè‚Äã
Working on a higher resolution display will improve your exam experience. 
‚óè‚Äã
Make sure your desktop is not ‚Äúzoomed in‚Äù or scaled.  This is common on smaller laptops. 
‚óè‚Äã
If you cannot use a high-resolution display, you can scale the browser to improve visibility and 
ensure all parts of the GUI can be seen or accessed. 
‚óè‚Äã
Copy and paste hot-keys will not work. Use your mouse for copy and paste. 
‚óè‚Äã
Be familiar with Adobe Acrobat's Advanced Search function to search an entire folder of PDFs at 
once. 
‚óè‚Äã
While Postman is provided, you may find it easier to complete API items using the REST API 
Explorer for that component. All API items can be completed using the API Explorer. 
‚óè‚Äã
If you see an alert in a cluster or a configuration change you think should be made ‚Äì but is not 
covered by a specific point in a scenario ‚Äì do not change it. Only make changes as directed in the 
scenarios on the specific cluster or component referenced by the scenario. 
‚óè‚Äã
We suggest you complete the items in order.  If you want to skip around, please use Notepad in 
the environment to track which scenarios you have completed.  The interface will indicate if you 
have viewed a scenario but there is no indication if you completed it.  You can also use the flag 
ability in the interface to note scenarios to review at the end. 
‚óè‚Äã
Partial credit will be given for each scenario, but scoring is performed after the exam is 
completed. If a scenario specifies to not reboot a component (and there is a scoring point 
associated with not rebooting) if you reboot that component when working on another portion 
of the exam, you will not be awarded the point. 
‚óè‚Äã
Make sure you read any notes included with the scenarios as they may have tips that take the 
environment design into consideration.
